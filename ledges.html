The Film

Two people.
One conversation.

Ledges is a short film about the kind of moment that changes everything — when two people find themselves on the edge of something they can't name, and have to decide whether to step back or lean in.

Shot on location in Los Angeles, the film is defined by its performances and the charged space between them. Giovanni Doray wrote, directed, and produced the film, casting Justin Tyler Kem and Jennifer Michele De Winters through Breakdown Express.

"Some conversations only happen once. This is one of them."

Director's Note

From the filmmaker

Ledges came from a question I kept returning to: what happens in the space just before a decision? Not the decision itself — but the moment you're standing on the edge of it, aware of everything you're about to change.

I wanted to make a film that lived entirely in that space. Two people, one location, one conversation that neither of them fully finishes. Justin and Jennifer brought something to this that I couldn't have written — a kind of honesty that only happens when actors are given room to breathe.

We shot it in Los Angeles. We had one day.
