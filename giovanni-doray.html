The Filmmaker

Stories born from
shadow & necessity

Giovanni Doray is a Vancouver-based writer, director, and producer whose work is shaped by urgency, instinct, and an unshakeable need to tell the truth. Of South Asian heritage — his grandfather's birth certificate reads British-Raj/India — Giovanni carries the weight of a history that was never given a story, and spends his work giving it one.

He writes scripts on overnight shifts and films them in Los Angeles on a $70 roundtrip ticket. He stays at a hostel on Melrose, casts through Breakdown Express, and rewrites to fit the voices of the actors he finds. His process is lean, deliberate, and deeply human.

His films — Ledges (2025), Once, In Palm Springs, and Paper Bride — are available on IMDb. Larger projects are in development: stories that will require studio backing but have always existed, waiting on a shelf for the right moment.

"I take little sprinkles of darkness and try to give them a home. If I can do that, I never have to fall into the dark myself."
